## Preamble

The Temple of Zeus issues this Declaration to place beyond all doubt, beyond all ambiguity, and beyond all future misrepresentation, the foundation of this institution, the identity of its Founder, the substance of its doctrine, and the sovereignty of its existence as a religious body answerable to the Ancient Gods alone.

This Declaration is not issued in response to a charge. It is not a defence. It is not a request to be permitted to define ourselves. The right of an institution to declare its own identity is anterior to the existence of any party that would dispute it. We do not negotiate that right. We exercise it.

Where misrepresentation has been circulated by encyclopedic sources, by academic compendia, by hostile secondary parties, and by careless writers reproducing one another without verification, this Declaration stands as the authoritative reply, written by the institution about itself, signed by its Founder, dated, and made permanent.

We issue this Declaration from the same authority by which we issued the [Declaration of Principles](DeclarationOfPrinciples.php) and the [Declaration on Political Severance:](DeclarationOnPoliticalSeverance.php) the authority of a sovereign religious institution operating under the Law of Ma'at, answerable to the Ancient Gods and to the Truth, and to no encyclopedic editor, no academic compiler, no political faction, and no temporal critic of any description.

The Temple of Zeus is a religious body. It is the contemporary institution of Zevism. It carries the name of Zeus and the authority of the Ancient Gods. It is what it declares itself to be, and it answers to nothing else.

## [#](#article_1)Article I: The Foundation

### I.I: The Communal Inheritance

A community existed from the year 2002 onward, formed by individuals who held the Ancient Gods in honour and who pursued, in scattered and incomplete fashion, the recovery of pre-Abrahamic spiritual knowledge. That community produced writings, gathered members, and sustained itself across the early decades of the 21st century, on platforms operating in the open internet.

That community was never a religious institution in the formal sense. It held no legal foundation. It registered no copyrights. It filed no trademarks. It convened no codified hierarchy. It was a longing of those who sensed the truth of the Ancient Gods, an aimless current of devotion that drifted without crystallisation. The community was social in its character, never institutional. It was a beginning, never an arrival.

The original organising figure of that community has passed. Her work, where it was good, served as one input among many to the recovery of the Ancient Faiths. Her work, where it was political, ideological, or contaminated by the prejudices of her time, was incompatible with the spiritual restoration the community was reaching for. The community endured because the Gods endured. The materials produced under that earlier name were never canonised, never legally protected, never institutionally sealed. They were the labour of a longing, and they remained the labour of a longing for as long as that period lasted.

It is, in retrospect, a mercy that no formal religious body was ever instituted under the prior conditions. A religious institution founded upon political contamination is not a religious institution at all; it is the political contamination wearing religious dress. The crystallisation of the community into an institution waited for the conditions of its rectification. Those conditions have now arrived.

The Temple of Zeus inherits the social continuity of that community. We honour the longing that gave rise to it. We honour the devotion of those who sustained it through years when no formal home for the work existed. We do not, however, inherit the political contaminations, the doctrinal errors, the unauthorised theological speculations, or the polemical excesses of that period. Those remain with the period that produced them. The Temple of Zeus has been founded on the rectified ground.

> The community was the seed; the Temple is what grew from it after the soil was cleared.

In the year MMXXVI, the Temple of Zeus was instituted as a sovereign religious body under the legal and theological authority of its Founder. The institution founded in this year does not continue any prior legal entity, because no prior legal entity ever existed in this lineage of work. The Temple of Zeus is the first and only legally constituted religious body in this lineage.

Two dates therefore stand in the institutional history of the Temple of Zeus. The first is 2002, the year of the community's origin as an informal social grouping with no legal existence. The second is MMXXVI (2026), the year of the Temple's founding as the legal religious institution that now bears the name. The first marks a longing. The second marks the answer to it. Both are honoured. They are not confused with one another, and they shall not be conflated by any party that wishes to write accurately about this institution.

The institutional founding is the act by which the spiritual aspirations of the preceding community are crystallised into a sovereign religious body, with codified doctrine, sealed scripture, registered marks, and a single Founder of legal record. Before MMXXVI, there was a longing without a Temple. From MMXXVI forward, there is a Temple. The distinction is not a matter of opinion. It is a matter of institutional fact.

> Two dates. One Temple. The longing of the first answered by the founding of the second.

## [#](#article_2)Article II: The Identity of Zevism

Zevism is the religion of the Temple of Zeus. We define it here, on the authority of the institution that bears its name, in the words of its Founder, in the form that shall stand as the canonical self-description of this religion in all encyclopedic, academic, and public reference.

We honour these traditions as branches of a single tree. We worship the Ancient Gods in all the forms They have assumed under the languages, climates, and peoples to whom They have manifested.

We do not adopt the modern academic classification "polytheism" as our self-description. That term is a category invented by Abrahamic and post-Abrahamic compilers to demarcate themselves from the elder faiths. The Ancient Gods existed before the term was coined, and They will exist long after the term has fallen out of academic fashion. We worship the Ancient Gods. They are many in form and one in source. The classifications of the academy do not bind the Gods, and we shall not bind ourselves to those classifications.

Zevism is not a "new religious movement." It is the contemporary institutional reconstitution of the religion that preceded every "movement" that the academic literature catalogues. Its sources are older than the discipline of religious studies. Its Gods are older than the institutions that study Them. The label "new religious movement" describes the institutional founding of MMXXVI; it does not describe the religion that is being instituted.

> Zevism is the Original Religion of Mankind. It answers to no taxonomic system that postdates it.

The supreme deity of Zevism is Zeus, who is the same God known across the elder pantheons under the names by which He revealed Himself to each people. The unity of the Sky-Father across the elder traditions is one of the foundational doctrines of Zevism. He has gone by many names. He is one God.

Zeus is the primary ruler of the Gods and of mankind. > Zeus is the Original Dyeus Pater. He has been worshipped under many names across many ages. He is one God, one Sovereign, one Sky-Father, across all the eras that have known Him.

The Temple of Zeus does not classify itself as Satanism. The label "Satanism" is, in its origin and its semantics, a derivative category of Abrahamic religion. The category was extended by Christianity to label every deity, faith, and figure that the Christian polemic sought to demonise. Whatever stood against Judaism, Christianity, or Islam, the Abrahamic literature called "Satanic." The label was a weapon, forged in polemic, applied to whomever the polemic sought to silence.

Two communities have, in modern times, accepted this Abrahamic label as a self-description. The first is the rationalist-atheist Satanism of the 20th century, which adopted the figure as a literary symbol of opposition to Christian moralism. The second is theistic Satanism, which inverted the Christian polemic and worshipped the figure that Christianity had named. Both are reactions to Abrahamism. Both presuppose the Christian framework they engage with. Neither has any relation to the elder faiths.

The Temple of Zeus does not worship "Satan." We do not invoke "Satan." We do not recognise the existence of "Satan" as anything other than a Hebrew polemical title imposed upon the Ancient Gods by their detractors, and inherited by Christianity and Islam as a category for whatever they wished to anathematise. To call Zeus "Satan" is to perform the Abrahamic inversion in reverse: to accept the polemical re-naming as if it carried any authority over the eternal identity of the Gods. We do not accept the polemical re-naming. We do not engage with the inversion. We worship the Ancient Gods under Their own eternal names, in Their own original identities.

The label "Satanism," when applied to the Temple of Zeus by encyclopedic editors or academic compilers, is therefore an error. It does not describe what we are. It translates our identity into a category that postdates us, a category defined by a religion that came after us, a category weaponised against the elder faiths from which we descend. We reject the label not because it offends us but because it is wrong on its own terms. The Original Faith cannot be classified by the categories of the religion that displaced it. The chronology forbids it. The semantics forbid it. The truth forbids it.

> Whatever has stood against Judaism, Christianity, and Islam, those religions have called "Satanism." The Temple of Zeus predates all three. The Original Faith does not require their categories.

The doctrine of the Temple of Zeus does not include the worship or theology of "Reptilians," "Greys," or any race of hostile or benevolent extraterrestrial beings. The framework of "ancient astronauts," as developed by 20th-century popular speculation and propagated through derivative literature, is not the framework of Zevism. We do not derive our theology from such speculations. We do not present the Gods as advanced extraterrestrial races, biological in their nature, finite in their power, and subject to the categories of evolutionary biology.

The Gods of Zevism are eternal intelligences. They exist beyond the material order. They may, when They choose, assume forms within the material world for Their own purposes, but Their being is not exhausted by any such form. The reduction of the eternal Gods to "advanced extraterrestrial beings who do not age" is a category error of the same kind as the reduction of religion to politics: it translates the eternal into the temporal and loses the substance entirely. We do not perform this translation. We worship the Gods as the eternal intelligences They are.

The question of whether other forms of intelligent life exist elsewhere in the cosmos is not the central concern of Zevism. We do not deny the possibility, but we do not derive our theology from the question, and we do not equate the Gods with whatever such beings might be found to exist. The Gods stand above the order of created beings. They are not one species among others.

> The Gods are eternal intelligences. They are above the category of the alien. The category cannot contain Them.

### III.II: On Race, Supremacy, and the Meritocratic Order

The Temple of Zeus does not teach, has never institutionally taught, and shall never teach the spiritual or natural superiority of any race, ethnicity, or people over any other. The doctrine that humanity was differentially created by competing extraterrestrial races, with one race standing as "superior" and another as "inferior," is foreign to Zevism. We repudiate it without qualification, without softening, without reservation, and without the slightest acceptance of any of its premises.

Zevism teaches the meritocratic order. Human beings are evaluated, in the cosmic accounting, by the degree of their alignment with *Ma'at* (cosmic order, justice, truth) and by their resistance to the spiritual pathologies catalogued in our doctrinal corpus, principally *Yehubor* (the bearing of sacred form emptied of its sacred content), *Birburim* (the chaotic disorder of the soul against itself), *Sahiburah* (the false sanctification of the corrupt), and *Izfet* (the cosmic disorder that opposes Ma'at). These are the categories by which souls are weighed. They apply to every person of every race, every nation, every tribe, every language, every ancestry. Race is not a spiritual category. Merit, alignment with Ma'at, and the practice of the Theophoric virtues are the spiritual categories.

We recognise that peoples and civilisations have, across history, performed differently in their cultivation of philosophy, science, art, and the spiritual sciences. We do not deny the historical record. We do not, however, attribute that performance to racial supremacy, to alien lineage, or to any biologically essentialist doctrine. We attribute it to the conditions, choices, and spiritual orientations of the peoples in question, and to the quality of the institutions they built or failed to build. Civilisations rise and fall by Theophoric and Yehuboric currents within them. They do not rise and fall by genetics.

On the question of any specific people: the Temple of Zeus regards every people of the earth as a tribe of mankind, common in origin, common in capacity, and common in their susceptibility to the spiritual pathologies and to the Theophoric virtues alike. Where any people, including any specifically named people of the present day, has organised itself under religious or ideological doctrines that elevate its own supremacy or that condemn its neighbours, we comment on the religious and ideological pathology, not on the people. The pathology is universal. It can appear in any tribe. The tribe is not the pathology.

Any individual of any race, any ethnicity, any national origin, any continent, any prior religious affiliation, who is aligned with Ma'at, who resists the spiritual pathologies, and who honours the Ancient Gods, is welcome in the Temple of Zeus. The Gods are not bound to a tribe. The Gods do not have a chosen people. The Gods recognise the Theophoric soul wherever the Theophoric soul appears.

> We rank by Ma'at, not by blood. The Gods do not have a chosen race. The Gods have those who honour Them.

Where the prior community used the grimoire names alongside the Ancient names, that practice is corrected in canonical Zevist doctrine. The grimoire layer is recognised for what it is: a falsification, a slander, a polemical artefact of the religion that displaced our own. We do not perpetuate the slander by repeating it.

We do not worship the "Goetic" figures as demons. We honour the Ancient Gods whose names the Goetic catalogue stole and inverted. Its concerns and aspirations were, in many cases, the seeds of the work that has now been crystallised into formal institution. We honour that history as history. We do not allow the social authority of that earlier period to claim doctrinal or legal standing over the institution that has now been founded. The institution has its own constituted authority. It defers to no other.

We anticipate the objections to this Declaration and answer them here.

The canonical works of the Temple of Zeus are published, dated, signed, and available. They contain no extraterrestrial mythology, no racial supremacism, no Goetic demonology, no liturgy directed to Abrahamic polemical figures, and no political affiliation. If you find any of these in the canonical doctrine, cite the chapter and verse. If you cannot, the rebrand allegation has no factual basis. The doctrine is what it is. Read it.
