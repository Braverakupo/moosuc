# About Guardian Daemons

## Socrates’s Demon: The Gods as Protectors in the Path to the Godhead

*by High Priest Zevios Metathronos*

“In the ancient world, Guardian Gods and Daemons were not seen as sinister forces but as luminous intermediaries between the divine and the mortal realms, personal guides entrusted with leading each soul toward its divine potential. The Greeks, Egyptians, and later the Neoplatonists believed that every human being was accompanied by a daimon, a tutelary spirit assigned by the Gods at birth.

This being was not merely a protector but a bridge of consciousness, whispering divine intuition, orchestrating synchronicities, and guiding the individual through trials designed to awaken remembrance of their higher nature.

Socrates spoke reverently of his daimonion, the inner voice that warned and directed him, revealing that to commune with one’s daemon was to commune with the will of the divine itself.

To the ancients, these Guardian Beings were catalysts of theosis, the process of becoming Godlike. By aligning with one’s daemon, one harmonized with the cosmic order and transcended ignorance and chaos. The daemon did not impose moral codes; it sculpted destiny through subtle impulses, awakening courage, discernment, and divine remembrance. In this sacred companionship, human life became an ascent, a gradual unveiling of the Godhead within. The mystic who recognized and obeyed their Guardian Spirit did not worship a distant deity but entered a living dialogue with the divine intelligence shaping all existence. Through the daemon’s guidance, the soul learned to mirror the Gods themselves: radiant, sovereign, and self-knowing.”

In the defence speech recorded by Plato in Apology, Socrates describes his so-called daimonion (Greek το δαιμόνιον) as a “superhuman thing” within him which from childhood onward intervened in his actions by preventing him from doing certain things, but never by urging him toward some course of action. He says:

“In the past the oracular \[mantikē\] art of the superhuman thing within me was in the habit of opposing me, every time, even about minor things, if I was going to do anything not correctly.” (Apology 31d)

This self-disclosure stands out in the ancient world because Socrates not only admitted the presence of a guiding spirit or sign, but also publicly declared that he consulted it — though always in the negative sense (what not to do) rather than in the positive sense (what to do). His accusers accused him of introducing “other daimones” in that he followed this inner voice rather than the conventional city gods.1

Turning to the broader tradition of daimones (sing. daimon) in the classical Greek worldview, we find that Socrates’ daimonion sits within a larger conceptual framework of intermediary spirits or divine signs that mediate between the divine realm and mortals. In the Symposium (202d–e) Plato puts these beings as “between the divine and the mortal” (literally μεταξύ θείας καὶ θνητῆς), saying of love that it is a great daimon because it links gods and men. The Orphic tradition likewise speaks of daimones not as wholly gods but as numinous agents in the cosmic economy of human becoming: the Orphic Hymns include a Hymn 73 “To Daimon” which venerates the protecting/divine power of a daimon a “good spirit” or guardian-power (ἀγαθός δαίμων) that orders and sustains life.2,3

In this light, Socrates’ daimonion can be read as the personal instance of the abiding pattern: a guardian spirit, a whisper of the divine, guiding the philosopher by negative admonitions, thereby preserving him for his vocation. It is striking that Socrates emphasised not that the sign told him what to do, but only what not to do, a God protecting with restraint that reinforces the notion of personal agency and rational enquiry, rather than surrendering to mystical commands. Moreover, by positioning this daimonion as present “from the earliest childhood” and as part of his soul’s constitution, he stakes the claim to a life oriented toward divine wisdom rather than mundane ambition. In sum: the ancient witnesses portray Socrates’ daimonion as a deeply personal yet traditional type of guiding spirit, one that aligns with the Orphic/Platonic schema of daimones as liminal forces—helping souls ascend from the human toward the divine.

### 1\. You do not choose your Guardian Daemon, they choose you.

They are far more advanced, wise, and psychic than we are, they know more than you do about yourself and what you need in order to advance. As you advance you will understand why your Guardian Daemon is more suited for you, rather than a Daemon you wanted to choose as your Guardian Daemon.

Just because you admire the traits or personality of a Daemon, does not mean they must be your Guardian Daemon. You can definitely keep other Daemons in mind as role models though!

### 2\. Everyone has a Guardian Daemon from the moment of dedication to Zeus.

Sometimes even before we dedicate, because they are highly psychic and know we are on the Path, or we may have already established a relationship with them from past lives.

Some members believed that you do not have a Guardian until you are psychic enough to spiritually communicate with them; this is false. Leading to the next point…

### 3\. You can communicate with your Guardian Daemon, even if you don’t know who He/She is yet!

Simply speak, either mentally or aloud, something like *“To my Guardian Daemon aligned with Zeus, \[say what you want to say\].”* Make sure you are focused. I did this often until I found out who mine was.

Doing this helps to establish a bond. As you are putting in effort to reach out to them, you are opening your mind and soul to them, and putting yourself on the path to knowing them and receiving guidance from them, even if you don’t know who they are yet.

### 4\. Some members think they do not need to find out who their Guardian Daemon is, or otherwise completely dismiss the idea of being guided by a Guardian Daemon.

These mindsets are actually caused by something psychological. Perhaps you did not get enough guidance from your parents or teachers while growing up, leading you to subconsciously believe that nobody can or will help you, that you must go it alone. Or perhaps you had bad advice from those you looked up to, causing you to feel like you must figure out everything on your own.

When advancing, there is only so far we can go alone. Your Guardian will be the one to advise certain things you should do, and to point out areas you need to work on, and give you advanced knowledge and lead you through the journey of advancement. Humans were not created to do everything by ourselves; we were created by Zeus to advance through Zeus!

As a bonus effect, when we develop a strong relation to such advanced beings, we are able to heal ourselves from various problems we may have. Connecting and bonding to your Guardian Daemon will help you better understand yourself and what is best for you. Your Guardian always wants what is best for you, to help you heal and advance to your fullest potential. (This is not to say that our Guardians show us everything or do everything for us, we still make our own lives.)

### 5\. Feeling drawn to a God does not necessarily mean they are your Guardian.

We feel drawn to the Gods because this is human nature; the Gods created us and were with us in the Ancient Days. Perhaps you’ve been to their temple in a past life, or they have attributes you admire and want for yourself. It’s human nature to want role models to look up to, who inspire us to do better.

The above is very important to understand because there are members who think they have many Guardians. Leading to the next point…

### 6\. Daemons often reach out to us, even if they are not our Guardian!

This is because they are interested in humanity and our development, and because they want us to remember them. I have had many of our Gods reach out to me over the years. When this happens, you have established a friendship with them; but this does not mean they are your Guardian Daemon, even if they help you with something. You can still honor them when you do a thanksgiving ritual on our holidays, for example.

### 7\. You might not feel drawn to your Guardian Daemon at first.

Not until a closer bond has been established. This means that if you look through the list of Gods and Goddesses, your Guardian Daemon might actually be one that you do not yet feel drawn to. The reason for this could have something to do with the next point…

### 8\. For those of you who have had much difficulty in finding out who your Guardian Daemon is:

There could be a blockage in your soul or mind that needs to be worked on. Ask to be led to finding out what you need to work on, in order to find out who your Guardian Daemon is. It will all be worth the time and effort once the issue is resolved. You will also become more healed and advanced as a result.

Another factor is that many people these days are always in a rush. Set aside some time every week to sit quietly and be open to receiving a sign from your Guardian.

And make sure your chakras are clean! Dirty chakras keep us back in every way.

### 9\. You cannot tell who your Guardian Daemon is by your birth chart.

Sometimes it is shown, but quite often it is not.

### 10\. Your Guardian Daemon is not based on your gender or sexual orientation.

For many people, their Guardian Daemon might be of the opposite gender. Or it might be based on which gender you feel more comfortable in the company of. Or it might be based on other factors. Regardless, there is no set rule for what your Guardian’s gender will be, and your sexual orientation does not make a difference either.

### 11\. Your Guardian Daemon is not about your ego.

Some members only want one of the Crowned Princes to be their Guardian Daemon, in order to feel more important. This is not how it works. All the Daemons, even the lower ranking ones, are still incredibly more advanced and powerful than humans are. You are blessed and fortunate to have whomever your Guardian might be!

### 12\. Who your Guardian is might change.

Some members might have the same Guardian Daemon for their entire lives; some members might have one Guardian Daemon for a certain period of time, and another Guardian Daemon for a different stage of their advancement. This is completely individual and up to Satan and our Guardians themselves. Some Guardians specialize in newer members, some specialize in members who have certain things to work on for a certain stage of their advancement, and so on.

Regardless, the Guardian you have at the time is perfect for you for that time. It is all carefully planned and considered, so there is no need to worry about anything.

### 13\. Don’t disregard the lower ranking Daemons of the races of Bes and Pazuzu!

These Daemons are protectors, guardians, friends to humanity, and often have more time for us than the main Gods. They can help give us information regarding various things in life. They also act as messengers for the main Gods.

I personally have a very good Daemon Friend of the same race as Bes, and he always comes to my aid when needed. When one of the God Rituals came out, as I was doing the ritual for the first time, an enemy entity came to me. My Daemon Friend immediately showed up and chased it off so I could continue the ritual uninterrupted.

Hail to all of our Guardians who guide us!
