# Melek Taus: The Inverted God of the Yezidis

Among the most revealing examples of a culture that has resisted assimilation from the Abrahamic sects is the religion of Yezidism.

The Yezidis, a tribe that was called “The Whites of the Middle East”, are a religious sect that has “deviated” from the traditional Islamic narrative of the Region but predates them also.

In accordance with their doctrines, they mention that “Melek Tausi” is actually the bringer of Truth, Illumination, and the King of the Heavenly universe. They see no evil in His figure, only goodness, and they consider Him the highest hierarchy that is on par with “God”. According to Islamics, the Yezidis do worship “Iblis, Satan, The Devil” and they have been repeatedly attempted to be genocided based on this foul claim.

The Name of Melek Taus, is so sacred to the Yezidis that they believe that the moment one utters that Name, one is in direct communication with God. Therefore, they mostly call their Great God regardless and ignore the fact that to others, they “worship Satan”. They call him by the title Melek Taus, meaning “Highest Messenger”.

They are correct about that statement. These beliefs have made them a target for genocide by Islamic terrorists, who seek to exterminate their beliefs under the pretext that they are “Satanic”, a true statement in fact, but only an excuse to cause unnecessary violence.

From the self-definition of the Yezidis on their official website:

> ”Tawûsê Melek is often identified by Muslims and Christians with Shaitan (Satan). The Yezidis, however, believe Tawûsê Melek is not a source of evil or wickedness. They consider him to be the leader of the archangels, not a fallen angel. They are forbidden from speaking the name Shaitan. In doing so, one creates an equal partner to God. God is the creator and the biggest force, and it is wrong to say that there is an evil force and that it is equal to God.
> 
> Yazidis believe that good and evil both exist in the mind and spirit of human beings. It depends on the humans, themselves, as to which they choose. In this process, their devotion to Tawûsê Melek is essential, since it was he who was given the same choice between good and evil by God, and chose the good.” 1

The Holy Book of Yezidism, the [Al Jilwah,](The_Al_Jilwah.php) namely, the “Black Book of Melek Ta'us”, is an officially accepted Holy Book in Zevism, and we know truly that it was dictated by Him personally to enlightened people of the Yezidis.

The outlooks of the Yezidis are very interesting to study in that they show the corruptions and interpolations that are false in the Yehuborim, Islamic, and Christian accounts.
