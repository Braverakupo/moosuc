# Spiritual Warfare Tactics and Strategies

We are making impact. Our work (both spiritual and informational) is reaching people, shifting consciousness, and weakening the hold of corrupted religious programs on humanity. We must work relentlessly and keep up the momentum. A warrior does not relax until the battle is truly won.

There are many tactics for effectively fighting the spiritual and cultural war against the Abrahamic programs. Here are some of the most effective approaches. This is serious work. Input from all Zevists is welcome.

## Information Warfare

The single most powerful weapon we have is **information**. The saying "The pen is mightier than the sword" is true, but with a monster of this scale, we must use every tool available. Information has been our greatest ally and has more influence than most would believe.

Use the internet. There are thousands of forums, discussion boards, social media groups, and online communities where questions about religion, spirituality, and ancient traditions are discussed daily. Your presence matters. A single well-placed piece of truth can shift someone's entire worldview.

When you encounter people discussing religion, especially those questioning Christianity or Islam, share your knowledge. Let them know that the original Pagan traditions are not about blood sacrifice, devil worship, or any of the other lies they have been fed. Direct them to the Temple of Zeus and our educational resources. Most people have never heard the truth. When they do, many of them recognize it immediately.

When engaging online, be professional and articulate. Present facts, not emotional outbursts. Copy and paste relevant information where appropriate. People are more inclined to read what is directly in front of them than to click on a link, though both are effective.

## Standing Your Ground in Daily Life

If you encounter Christian proselytizing in retail stores, businesses, waiting rooms, or any other public space, **complain professionally**. Christians have operated with no opposition for centuries. Make sure you address the manager or whoever is in authority.

Be firm and to the point. Do not raise your voice, get emotional, or use aggressive language. If you fail to get a positive response, note the name of the manager, the date, and take the matter to someone in higher authority such as a district manager. Let them know you are offended by the religious imposition and will no longer do business with them if they do not act to correct the situation. Write everything down.

Remaining silent in the face of religious imposition is a way of condoning it. Professional, dignified pushback is what changes institutional behavior.
